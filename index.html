Start the Conversation

Casual opener for health checks — not a pitch
✕
When to use this

During routine health checks, QBRs, or catch-ups with customers using both ELMO and Datapay. Gauge interest — don't sell. If curious, move to Step 2.

What to say
"We've been working on a partnership between ELMO and Datapay to connect HR and payroll more tightly. Instead of managing data in two systems separately, there's now a way to sync employee records automatically — new hires and pay changes — all in real time. It's early days, but for the right customers it can save a lot of double-handling. Would it be worth a 20-minute look at how it works?"
What to listen for
  • "We've been talking about that internally"
  • "Who would I need to involve?"
  • "What data does it sync?"
  • "We've had issues with duplicate entry"
  • "How long would it take?"
What not to do
  • Don't pitch pricing or scope.
  • Don't demo or go technical.
  • Don't commit to timelines.
  • Don't oversell. Not interested? Revisit next quarter.
If they're interested
  1. Log in Partner Portal
  2. Run Qualification Tool (Step 2)
  3. Only then schedule validation call (Step 3)

Handle Customer Objections

4 pushbacks · Click i for detail
✕
💬
"CSV works fine — why pay more?"
Acknowledge it works. API V2 removes the human effort around CSV.
Why
Working CSV — often years old. Real cost hidden: reconciliation, errors, key-person risk.
Approach
Don't dismiss CSV. Shift to what's changed. No pain? Say "not yet."
Say
"CSV has been solid. API V2 is for when volume, compliance, or double-handling creates real cost. If that's not you yet, we'd rather wait."
💬
"API doesn't cover everything CSV does."
Be honest. If gaps create more work than API saves, don't push.
Why
~60% coverage vs CSV. Informed buyers spot gaps — good sign.
Approach
Find where covered fields = bulk of pain. Don't force a square peg.
Say
"API covers the core lifecycle. For some, that's 90% of value. For others, gaps matter more. We'd rather be upfront."
💬
"We're too busy for another project."
4–6 weeks. Live payroll never touched.
Why
Perceived effort, not actual. "Integration project" = disruption in their mind.
Approach
Pay runs unaffected. Replica test env. Customer = validation only. 2 weeks post-live support.
Say
"4–6 weeks. Live environment never touched. Heavy lifting is on our side."
💬
"Why two contracts / two vendors?"
Specialist accountability. Coordinated behind the scenes.
Why
Two vendors = complexity fear. Managing a boundary.
Approach
Complexity behind the scenes. "All-in-one" = shallow NZ payroll.
Say
"Two specialists, not one generalist. We coordinate so you don't manage the boundary."
